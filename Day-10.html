Macro 2 — Inflation, Jobs & Policy

CPI and inflation, unemployment and the labour force, and the two policy levers.

⏸ Before you listen - guess first

A central bank is worried prices are rising too fast. Should it make borrowing cheaper, or more expensive?

Decide, then press play.

🎧 Listen ~6 min

Play the episode, then work through the question below.

  • Recession - fight it with EXPANSIONARY policy — more government spending or lower taxes, and lower interest rates — to lift demand.
  • High inflation - fight it with CONTRACTIONARY policy — less spending or higher taxes, and higher interest rates — to cool demand.

The unemployment rate divides by the labour force (employed + those looking), never by the whole population.

🔢 Worked example

Part 1 · The question

In a country the working-age population is 8 (million). Of those, 5 are employed and 1 is unemployed but actively looking for work; the rest are not looking. What are the labour-force participation rate and the unemployment rate?

Part 2 · Solve it step by step

Labour force = employed + unemployed (looking). Participation = labour force ÷ working-age population. Unemployment = unemployed ÷ labour force.
1
Labour force.
5 + 1 = 6
2
Participation rate.
6 ÷ 8 = 75%
3
Unemployment rate.
1 ÷ 6 ≈ 16.7%
4
Note the trap.
We divided by the labour force (6), not the population (8).
✓ Answer: Participation rate 75%; unemployment rate about 16.7%.

Now you try - reveal only after you work it out

A basket cost 100 last year (CPI 100) and 102 this year. What is the inflation rate?
About 2%. Inflation = (102 − 100) ÷ 100 × 100 = 2%.
A retired person is not working and not looking for work. Are they unemployed?
No — they are outside the labour force entirely, so they don't count as unemployed.
