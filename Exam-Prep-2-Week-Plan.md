# Introduction to Economics — 2-Week Exam Prep Plan

Bar Ilan IMBA, Spring 2026 · Dr. Andrew Schein
Built for ~30 minutes a day. Podcasts on days 1–10, then practice and mock exams on days 11–14.

## What the exam looks like

Based on the past finals in your project (2024, 2025 Moed Aleph, 2025 Moed Bet):

- 25 multiple-choice questions, 4 points each, 100 points total
- 2 hours, closed book, simple calculator allowed
- A formula sheet is given to you at the end of the test
- Two kinds of questions mixed together:
  - **Calculations** — GDP (nominal, real, per capita), CPI and inflation, price elasticity, unemployment and labor force rates, profit and cost tables
  - **Reasoning / "what happens to the graph"** — supply and demand shifts, price ceilings and floors, subsidies, who gains and who loses, short run vs long run

Roughly half the questions are micro, half are macro.

## How each day works

Each podcast day (1–10):
1. Listen to the short podcast (~8–10 min) — one topic, plain English.
2. Do the matching interactive quiz (a few exam-style questions with instant feedback and worked solutions).
3. Note anything that felt shaky — we tighten it on the review days.

## Key formulas you'll be drilled on

- **GDP** = C + I + G + NX. Nominal uses current-year prices; real uses base-year prices.
- **Real GDP per capita** = Real GDP ÷ population (the standard-of-living measure).
- **CPI** = (cost of basket this year ÷ cost of basket in base year) × 100.
- **Inflation rate** = (CPI this year − CPI last year) ÷ CPI last year × 100.
- **Price elasticity of demand** = % change in quantity ÷ % change in price (a negative number).
- **% change in quantity** = elasticity × % change in price.
- **Labor force** = employed + unemployed (looking for work).
- **Participation rate** = labor force ÷ working-age population × 100.
- **Unemployment rate** = unemployed ÷ labor force × 100.
- **Profit** = TR − TC = (P × Q) − TC.
- **Cost tables**: TC = FC + VC; ATC = TC ÷ Q; AVC = VC ÷ Q; MC = change in TC from one unit to the next (add it row by row — don't subtract).
- **Shutdown (short run)**: produce if P ≥ AVC. **Exit (long run)**: stay only if P ≥ ATC.

## Two traps from the past finals to watch

- The **shutdown test** (P vs AVC) and the **profit test** (TR vs TC) answer *different* questions. A firm can lose money and still be right to keep producing in the short run.
- "**It cannot be known**" is often the correct answer when *both* supply and demand shift in opposite directions — the price moves a known way but the quantity does not (or vice versa).
